Research


The study of the dynamics of dust and debris from small celestial bodies is crucial for understanding the early distribution and processes within the protoplanetary disk of our solar system, mitigating collision risks for space missions, comprehending material exchange between stellar and planetary systems, assessing the impact risks of meteoritic debris entering Earth's atmosphere, and interpreting astronomical observations affected by scattered sunlight from these particles, which together enhance our knowledge of cosmic phenomena, protect our planet, and aid future space exploration and technological developments.

1. Liu, B., & Liu, X. (2024). Unraveling the dust activity of naked-eye comet C/2022 E3 (ZTF). Astronomy & Astrophysics, 683, A51

2. Liu, B., Liu, X., Jia, X., Li, F., Zhao, Y., & Yu, L. (2023). Active asteroid 311P/PanSTARRS: Rotational instability as the origin of its multitails?. The Astronomical Journal, 166(4), 156.

3. Liu, J.,Liu, B., Wu, Z., Jiang, J., \& Tian, L. (2020). Dynamics and potential applications of a lunar space tethered system. Acta Astronautica, 169, 138-149.

4. Liu, B., & Liu, X. Physical Property of Newly Active Asteroid 2010 LH15. The Astrophysical Journal Letters, Under Review.
